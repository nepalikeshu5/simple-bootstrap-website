How Cocoa Chocolate ?

The chocolate production process consists of fermentation, drying, roasting, grinding of cocoa beans, mixing of all ingredients like cocoa mass, sugar, cocoa butter, emulsifiers, aroma, and milk

What is Cocoa Bean ?

Cocoa beans are the dried and fully fermented seeds of the cacao tree (Theobroma cacao). The cacao tree is native to the deep tropical regions of Central and South America, but it is also cultivated in other parts of the world with suitable climates, including West Africa, Southeast Asia, and some islands in the Pacific.

How They Harvest ?

Cocoa beans are harvested from cacao trees, primarily grown in tropical regions. The process begins with the manual harvesting of large, colorful cacao pods containing seeds. Farmers carefully cut the ripe pods from the trees, avoiding damage to the surrounding foliage.

Process of Harvesting

Cocoa beans are harvested from cacao trees. Ripe pods are manually collected, opened, and the beans extracted from the sweet pulp. After extraction, beans undergo fermentation, drying for a week and roasting for flavor development.
